Tech Hero

Michael Running Wolf

Michael Running Wolf is an Indigenous technologist and researcher who pushes back against “one-size-fits-all” AI by centering Indigenous communities, data ownership, and language preservation. His work highlights a basic truth in this class: technology is never neutral—what we build reflects whose knowledge is valued, whose voices are included, and who gets to control the tools.

One reason he stands out is that he focuses on language technology for Indigenous languages—areas that big tech often ignores because they are not “profitable enough” or don’t have massive datasets. Instead of treating communities as data sources, his approach emphasizes consent, collaboration, and protecting cultural knowledge. That flips the usual power dynamic where institutions extract data and communities receive little benefit.

Running Wolf is also known for leadership and public education: he’s spoken about ethics, representation, and what it means to build technology responsibly while respecting Indigenous sovereignty. In a space where marginalized people are often asked to “fit” existing systems, his work challenges the systems themselves—especially around who AI is for and who gets harmed when bias is baked into tools.
